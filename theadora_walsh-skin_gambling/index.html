Skin Gambling

and digital augmentation

Last April, Travis Scott held a virtual concert inside the video game Fortnite. The pandemic has been good for the free to play online game. In April alone, Fortnite made $400 million dollars, a huge jump in revenue, and players logged over 3.2 billion hours in the battle royale shooter.

Even though gaming is ubiquitous, with 70% of people under 18 self-reporting that they play, the world is occluded to outsiders.

“If I take you to my past you will be traumatized” Scott sings in the Fortnite rendition of “stargazing”.

Youtube conversations about skin gambling occur mostly between teenagers and boys in their early twenties. They talk a lot about “flexing.”

Flexing

  1. The bending of a joint.
  2. To put a (skill, talent, or ability) to use

It’s a direct commodification of social capital, one that can be turned into currency, and abstraction, and betted against to generate huge revenues.
